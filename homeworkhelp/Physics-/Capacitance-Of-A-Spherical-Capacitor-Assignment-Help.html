Capacitance of a Spherical Capacitor (Inner Sphere Earthed)


A and B are two spheres of radii a and b. suppose a charge + q is given to the outer sphere B. +q is distributed on its inner an outer surfaces by amounts +q1 and +q2 respectively, so that q = q1 + q2. The charge + q1 on the inner surface of B induces a charge –q1 on the inner surface of B induces a charge – q1 (bound charge) on the outer surface of A and charge + q1 on the inner surface of A. being free, leaks to the earth.

The two spheres now behave as two capacitors connected in parallel.

(i) The inner sphere of radius a and the inner surface of outer sphere form a capacitor of capacitance

        C1 = 4πε0ab    (if the dielectric in air)
                   b – a

(ii) The outer surface of B and the earth form a capacitor of capacitance
        C2 = 4πε0ab.

Total capacitance C = C1 + C2 = 4πε0ab + 4πε0b
                    _________________(b – a)

            C = 4πε0b2
                     b – a
